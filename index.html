Understanding the ICE Framework

Component Breakdown

üéØ Impact (1-10)

Definition: How much will this channel move the needle on your goals?

  • 10: Game-changing potential, directly addresses target audience
  • 7-9: High potential, strong audience match
  • 4-6: Moderate impact, some audience overlap
  • 1-3: Low impact, minimal audience match

üí™ Confidence (1-10)

Definition: How certain are you that this channel will deliver results?

  • 10: Proven track record, extensive data available
  • 7-9: Strong indicators, some case studies
  • 4-6: Mixed signals, limited data
  • 1-3: Unproven, purely speculative

‚ö° Ease (1-10)

Definition: How simple is it to implement and manage this channel?

  • 10: Plug-and-play, minimal setup required
  • 7-9: Straightforward, some learning curve
  • 4-6: Moderate complexity, requires expertise
  • 1-3: Very complex, significant resources needed

Strategic Budget Allocation

Allocation Methods

Method 1: ICE-Weighted Distribution

Allocate budget proportionally based on ICE scores

Channel Budget = Total Budget √ó (Channel ICE / Sum of All ICE Scores)

Method 2: Risk-Adjusted Allocation

Balance high-performing channels with experimental ones

  • 70% to proven channels (ICE > 7)
  • 20% to promising channels (ICE 5-7)
  • 10% to experimental channels (ICE < 5)

Method 3: Objective-Based Allocation

Distribute based on campaign goals

  • Awareness: Favor reach-heavy platforms
  • Conversion: Prioritize high-intent channels
  • Engagement: Focus on interactive platforms
